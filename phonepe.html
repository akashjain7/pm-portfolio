Key observation: PhonePe derives ~56% of its volume from Tier 2/3 cities. A post-Diwali TSR dip in this segment is not just a UX issue. It directly threatens retention of its highest-growth user base, risking churn to Google Pay and Paytm.
PM framing: "Transaction success rate (TSR) among Tier 2 users dropped by ~8% in the week following Diwali 2024. I want to understand whether this is a product problem, an infrastructure problem, or an external problem and which layer to fix first."
Diagnostic framework: issue tree
Breaking the metric drop into layers helps avoid jumping to solutions. Each branch must be ruled in or out with data before forming hypotheses.

Segmentation narrows the problem: the dip is concentrated among Tier 2 new users making merchant payments. This rules out systemic infrastructure failure (which would affect P2P too) and points to a UX + connectivity combination for first-time users in merchant-dense contexts.
Each hypothesis is rated by likelihood (based on public data + Tier 2 behaviour patterns) and ease of validation.
› H1 · Network congestion + connectivity drops
High likelihood
Why
Tier 2 cities have significantly weaker 4G/5G coverage than metro areas. During Diwali, network load spikes dramatically due to WhatsApp, video calls, and social media. UPI API calls get deprioritised or time out mid-transaction.
Supporting data: NPCI acknowledged intermittent outages in March–April 2025 due to overloaded systems. Tier 2 connectivity challenges are well-documented.
Fix
UPI Lite (offline mode for <₹500), retry-with-confirmation UX, pending transaction status with estimated resolution time.
› H2 · First-time user friction in merchant flow
High likelihood
Why
PhonePe ran aggressive Diwali acquisition campaigns in Tier 2, bringing in users who had never transacted before. These users frequently enter wrong PINs, misread QR codes, or panic and abandon mid-flow, all counted as failures.
Supporting data: New user TSR is 87.3% vs 94.1% for existing Tier 2 users in our segmentation, a 7-point gap that directly explains the aggregate dip.
Fix
Contextual onboarding for first merchant transaction, guided QR scan with visual validation, vernacular language prompts, 1-tap retry with pre-filled details.
› H3 · Bank server overload (payer-side bank failures)
Medium likelihood
Why
Regional cooperative and small private banks dominant in Tier 2 cities often have limited server capacity. Diwali spikes can overload these banks' UPI endpoints, causing declines that look like PhonePe failures to the user.
Supporting data: Zero MDR policy means banks earn nothing per UPI transaction, reducing incentive to invest in infrastructure upgrades.
Fix
Proactive "your bank is experiencing high load" messaging rather than generic failure. Suggest adding a secondary bank account. Build bank health monitor into PhonePe's backend.
› H4 · Competitive promo-driven failed transaction attempts
Lower likelihood
Why
Paytm ran aggressive Diwali cashback campaigns targeting Tier 2 kirana merchants. Some users may have attempted PhonePe payments at merchants who only had Paytm QR codes visible, leading to mis-scans and failures.
Lower likelihood as primary cause. Paytm's market share is at 6.9%, limiting scale. But worth monitoring as part of competitive intelligence.
Fix
Merchant sticker campaigns reinforcing PhonePe acceptance, QR validation before payment attempt, real-time merchant app status indicator.
Hypothesis likelihood × ease of fix matrix
Primary root cause: A combination of network-driven timeouts (external, harder to fix) and first-time user friction in merchant payment flow (internal, directly actionable). The PM recommendation prioritises the internal fix first, with a faster impact, within PhonePe's control.
Three product interventions, prioritised using an Impact × Effort lens. All address the root causes identified in the RCA.
P1 · Ship in 2 weeks
Guided first-merchant-payment onboarding
A 3-step contextual overlay appears the first time a new Tier 2 user scans a merchant QR. Step 1: visual PIN confirmation. Step 2: amount review with merchant name. Step 3: success animation with refund policy reminder. Reduces abandonment and wrong-PIN failures.
High impact Low effort UX
P2 · Ship in 4 weeks
Smart failure messaging with auto-retry
Replace the generic "Payment failed" screen with context-aware messages: "Your bank is experiencing high load. We'll retry in 30 seconds." Auto-retry once; show estimated resolution time. If bank-side failure, surface the option to pay from a different linked bank account.
High impact Medium effort Infra + UX
P3 · Ship in 8 weeks
UPI Lite push for Tier 2 merchant payments
Proactively nudge Tier 2 users to enable UPI Lite (offline-capable, <₹500 per transaction). Run a Diwali-timed in-app campaign with vernacular explanation. Reduces network-dependency for small merchant payments, the highest failure-risk use case in this segment.
Medium impact Medium effort Product + Growth
