Zomato Quick Reorder

Problem statement

Repeat users are stuck in a discovery loop they didn't ask for

Zomato's core loop — open app → browse → decide → order — breaks at the "decide" step for a significant portion of users. Users who already know what they want are forced through the same discovery flow designed for first-time exploration.

Core hypothesis: ~40% of Zomato orders are repeats of a past order. These users spend an estimated 6–10 minutes re-navigating to the same restaurant and item they've ordered before, creating friction that directly suppresses order frequency and conversion rate.

Why this matters for Zomato's business

Zomato's North Star is total successful orders per day. Every minute added to the decision-to-checkout flow increases drop-off probability. For a platform doing ~2M+ orders/day, even a 2% improvement in repeat-user conversion is material revenue.

This is NOT a discovery problem
The existing recommendation engine and restaurant discovery feed work well for new exploration. Quick Reorder is a parallel track for users who already know what they want.
This IS a recall + speed problem
The current UI treats every session like the user's first visit. "Past Orders" exists but is buried 3 taps deep and shows full restaurant menus, not the specific items ordered.
User research

Who is most affected and what they're saying

Research based on App Store and Play Store review analysis, Reddit threads (r/india, r/zomato), and behavioural inference from product flows.

Three primary segments
💼
Working professionals: High frequency (daily)
Order lunch from the same 2–3 restaurants every weekday. Re-searching the same restaurant every time is the core pain point. Time is the constraint.
🎓
Students: Medium frequency (3–4x/week)
Late-night repeat orders from nearby cheap options. Browsing through irrelevant restaurants at 11pm when they just want what they had yesterday.
🏠
Families: Low-medium frequency (weekly)
Weekend biryani or pizza ritual orders. Long browse time due to menu size even when the decision is already made before opening the app.
Key insight from reviews
The top complaint pattern in 1–2 star reviews is not food quality or delivery time, it's "I just wanted to reorder and it took forever to find it." The "Past Orders" section exists but is buried 3 taps deep and shows full restaurant menus, not the specific items ordered.
Opportunity sizing

If 35–40% of orders are repeats, and average repeat-user browsing time is 8 minutes vs. 3 minutes for a direct reorder, reducing that gap by 50% frees ~2.5 minutes per session. Across 800K repeat orders/day, this is a meaningful conversion and satisfaction lever.

What users want
One tap to get back to what they had before. They're not looking for a recommendation; they're looking for recall. The decision is already made before they open the app.
What the current product gives them
A full discovery flow search, filter, browse, select restaurant, navigate menu, find item, add to cart. At least 5–7 taps for something they've done a dozen times.
The feature

Quick Reorder- contextual, predictive, one-tap

A contextual bottom sheet on the Zomato home screen that surfaces the user's most likely next order, specific dish + restaurant based on time of day, day of week, and order history. One tap adds to cart. Two taps place the order.

What makes it different from "Order Again": It's predictive, not reactive. It surfaces before the user searches at the moment they open the app, based on when they usually order this. It's not a list of past orders. It's the one right answer for this moment.
How the prediction works
ContextWhat Quick Reorder showsLogic
Weekday, 12–2pmButter Chicken + Naan from Punjabi Dhaba (₹280)Most ordered item, weekday lunch window
Weekday, 9–11pmChicken Burger from McDonald's (₹180)Late-night pattern, last 5 orders
Weekend, 7–9pmPepperoni Pizza from Domino's (₹499)Weekend dinner pattern
New user (cold start)Trending near you: Biryani from Apni DukaanLocation-based trending, no order history
Frequency capping

The sheet does not appear on every session, which turns a helpful nudge into an annoyance. It shows a maximum of 3x per week per user, and only when prediction confidence is high (3+ orders in the same time window). Low-confidence sessions fall back to the standard home screen.

Dismiss behaviour
Tap ✕ or overlay — session dismiss
Closes the sheet for this session only. The user might want it tomorrow. No permanent signal sent to the model.
"Don't show this again" — permanent opt-out
Permanent dismiss. Feeds back to the prediction model as a negative signal. Respects long-term preference vs. short-term mood.

RICE framework

FactorEstimateReasoning
Reach~8M users/monthZomato has ~20M MAU. ~40% place repeat orders = ~8M users in active windows.
Impact3 / 515–20% reduction in time-to-order; ~5–8% lift in repeat conversion. High-confidence incremental gain.
Confidence80%Amazon "Buy Again", Swiggy Instamart "Reorder" both show lift. Existing data. Low-complexity ML.
Effort3 weeks (M)Frontend: bottom sheet (~1w). Backend: time-based ranking model (~1.5w). A/B setup (~0.5w).
RICE Score = (8,000,000 × 3 × 0.8) / 3 = ~6.4M
High priority. Low risk. Uses existing data infrastructure. Targets the highest-frequency user cohort. No new data sources needed.

Risk analysis

What could go wrong and how we'd handle it

Cold start problem
New users (<3 orders) see no sheet or an irrelevant suggestion
Mitigation
Show trending/location-based suggestions for new users; don't show sheet at all for 0-order users
Restaurant availability
Suggested restaurant is closed or not delivering to user's current location
Mitigation
Real-time availability check before rendering sheet; show next best candidate if unavailable
Menu or price changes
Item price changed since last order; user feels misled at checkout
Mitigation
Always show live price on card, never cached. Flag if item is no longer available on menu
Reduces exploration
Users stop discovering new restaurants hurts long-term retention and LTV
Mitigation
Monitor new restaurant order rate as a guardrail metric; cap sheet to 3x/week max
Low prediction accuracy
Wrong suggestion shown → sheet feels useless → users dismiss permanently
Mitigation
Require minimum 3 orders in same time window before predicting; track permanent dismiss rate as health metric
Rollout plan

Phased launch

α
Alpha — Weeks 1–2
Internal team + 1,000 power users (5+ orders/month). Goal: validate prediction accuracy, catch edge cases, gather qualitative feedback on the bottom sheet UX.
β
Beta A/B — Weeks 3–4
10% of users with ≥3 order history. Measure CTR, time-to-order delta, and repeat conversion lift. Only proceed to full rollout if metrics hit targets at 95% confidence.
1.0
Full rollout — Weeks 5–6
All users with ≥3 orders, if A/B metrics hit targets. Monitor guardrail metrics (discovery rate, dismiss rate) for 2 weeks post-launch before declaring success.
Expected outcome: If A/B test shows a 5% increase in repeat-user conversion, the potential revenue gain across 800K+ daily repeat sessions makes full investment in the feature worthwhile and opens the door to more advanced personalization (multi-item prediction, cuisine-level affinity, loyalty integration) in v2.
